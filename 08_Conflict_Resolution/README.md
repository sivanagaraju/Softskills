Conflict resolution is the process by which two or more parties reach a peaceful solution to a dispute. In the workplace, conflict is inevitable; however, the ability to manage it constructively turns potential disasters into opportunities for innovation and stronger relationships.

## üìÅ Sub-Folder Overview

### [01_Foundations_of_Conflict](./01_Foundations_of_Conflict)

**Objective:** Understanding why conflict happens.

- Types of conflict: Task, Relationship, and Value-based.
- Identifying the early warning signs of escalation.
- Distinguishing between "Healthy Tension" and "Destructive Conflict."

### [02_Conflict_Management_Styles](./02_Conflict_Management_Styles)

**Objective:** Recognizing your default reaction to disputes.

- **The Thomas-Kilmann Model:** Competing, Collaborating, Compromising, Avoiding, and Accommodating.
- Knowing when to use each style depending on the situation.
- Shifting from "Winning" to "Problem-Solving."

### [03_Communication_in_Conflict](./03_Communication_in_Conflict)

**Objective:** Using language to lower the temperature.

- **"I" Statements:** Communicating needs without attacking or blaming.
- Avoiding "Universal" qualifiers (e.g., "You always..." or "You never...").
- The power of pause and reflective listening during a heated moment.

### [04_Interest_Based_Problem_Solving](./04_Interest_Based_Problem_Solving)

**Objective:** Moving from "Positions" to "Interests."

- **Interest-Based Relational (IBR) Approach:** Keeping people and problems separate.
- Finding common ground and "Win-Win" outcomes.
- Brainstorming options for mutual gain.

### [05_De_escalation_Techniques](./05_De_escalation_Techniques)

**Objective:** Handling high-emotion scenarios.

- Managing the "Amygdala Hijack" (self and others).
- Lowering vocal pitch and slowing down speech patterns.
- Creating a "time-out" or "cooling-off" period.

### [06_Mediation_and_Third_Party_Intervention](./06_Mediation_and_Third_Party_Intervention)

**Objective:** When and how to involve a neutral party.

- The role of a mediator (Listening, Framing, and Neutrality).
- Establishing ground rules for a tripartite discussion.
- Formal vs. Informal mediation in a team setting.

### [07_Post_Conflict_Recovery_and_Prevention](./07_Post_Conflict_Recovery_and_Prevention)

**Objective:** Repairing the relationship and moving forward.

- Trust rebuilding exercises.
- Creating "Conflict Agreements" for future interactions.
- Documenting resolutions to ensure follow-through.
