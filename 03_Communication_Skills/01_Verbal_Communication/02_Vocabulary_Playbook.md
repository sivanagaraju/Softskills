# Corporate Vocabulary Playbook

> Your daily cheat sheet. Every phrase here is organized by the situation you'll use it in — not alphabetically. Learn 2-3 per day and force yourself to use them.

---

## 1. Standup & Status Updates

Use these when your manager asks "What's the update?" — replace vague answers with precise, confident language.

### Power Phrases

| Instead of...       | Say... | Why It's Stronger |
|---------------------|--------|-------------------|
| "I'm working on it" | "I'm making progress on X and expect to **wrap up** by [date]" | Shows timeline awareness |
| "It's going fine"   | "We're **on track** to deliver by [date]" | Quantified progress |
| "I found a problem" | "I want to **flag** a potential risk with [X]" | Professional risk communication |
| "I'll talk to them" | "I'll **align** with [team/person] on this today" | Implies strategic coordination |
| "I'm almost done"   | "I'm **finalizing** the [deliverable] — targeting completion by EOD" | Precision over vagueness |
| "I'm stuck"         | "I've hit a **bottleneck** with [X] and need input from [person]" | Names the blocker, requests help |
| "I need help"       | "I need to **loop in** [person] to **unblock** this" | Action-oriented |
| "We should hurry"   | "We need to **accelerate** the timeline on this" | Professional urgency |
| "I'll check"        | "I'll **look into** that and **get back to you** by [time]" | Commits to follow-through |
| "Let me explain"    | "Let me **walk you through** the current status" | Signals structured thinking |

### Reporting Status with Confidence

In Agile environments, **"Blockers"** and **"Impediments"** are the standard, professional terms. Use them for brevity. If you want to sound extra proactive, you can add a "Momentum Detail."

| Situation | Standard (Effective) | Proactive Add-on (For Momentum) |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------------------------|
| Everything is fine | "No blockers." | "...I'm fully focused on [X] and on track for EOD." |
| No issues found | "No impediments." | "...I have a clear path forward for the rest of the week." |
| Finished a task | "I've wrapped up X." | "...and I'm now pivoting to Y to maintain velocity." |
| Waiting for info | "Pending info on X." | "...but continuing with Y so as not to hinder progress." |

### Key Verbs for Standups

| Verb            | Meaning                      | Example |
|-----------------|------------------------------|---------|
| **Streamline**  | Make more efficient          | "We **streamlined** the review process to cut turnaround by 2 days" |
| **Expedite**    | Speed up                     | "I'm working to **expedite** the approval" |
| **Consolidate** | Combine into one             | "I **consolidated** the three reports into a single dashboard" |
| **Delegate**    | Assign to others             | "I've **delegated** the testing to [name]" |
| **Prioritize**  | Rank by importance           | "I **prioritized** the client-facing bugs over internal ones" |
| **Validate**    | Confirm accuracy             | "I need to **validate** these numbers before sharing" |

### Useful Phrasal Verbs

- **Catch up on** — "I need to **catch up on** the backlog from last week"
- **Run through** — "Let me **run through** the key updates"
- **Follow up** — "I'll **follow up** with the vendor today"
- **Break down** — "I'll **break down** the tasks for the sprint"
- **Carry on** — "We'll **carry on** with the current approach unless told otherwise"

## 2. Client Communication

Use these for emails, calls, and meetings with clients. The pattern: acknowledge → explain → commit.

### Email/Message Templates

**Addressing a concern:**
> ❌ "We are working on it."
> ✅ "I understand your concern, and we're currently **addressing** it. Let me **outline** the steps we're taking to **resolve** it promptly."

**Sharing a delay:**
> ❌ "The project is delayed."
> ✅ "I wanted to **inform** you about an unexpected delay. Here's what caused it and the steps we're **implementing** to **mitigate** the impact."

**Requesting information:**
> ❌ "We need more info."
> ✅ "To move forward effectively, we **require** some additional details on [X]. This will help us **tailor** our approach to better meet your needs."

**Following up:**
> ❌ "Have you reviewed our proposal?"
> ✅ "I wanted to **follow up** on the proposal we shared last week. Are there any questions or points you'd like to **discuss** to support your decision-making process?"

**Delivering bad news:**
> ❌ "The results aren't what we expected."
> ✅ "Unfortunately, the results didn't meet our expectations. Let's **review** the data together and **identify** the next steps to improve outcomes."

### Client Power Words

| Word           | Use When... |
|----------------|------------|
| **Ensure**     | Promising a standard ("We'll **ensure** quality at every stage") |
| **Accommodate** | Being flexible ("We can **accommodate** that request") |
| **Tailor**     | Customizing ("We'll **tailor** the solution to your requirements") |
| **Anticipate** | Predicting needs ("We **anticipate** this will improve by Q3") |
| **Facilitate** | Making things easier ("I'll **facilitate** a call between both teams") |
| **Demonstrate** | Showing proof ("Let me **demonstrate** how this works") |

## 3. Disagreeing & Pushback

Use these when you need to say "no", challenge an idea, or push back professionally — without burning bridges.

### Polite Pushback Phrases

| Situation                    | What to Say |
|------------------------------|------------|
| Rejecting a timeline         | "I appreciate the urgency, but to **ensure** quality, we'd need to **adjust** the timeline to [date]" |
| Disagreeing with an approach | "I see the reasoning, but I'd like to **put forward** an alternative that might **mitigate** the risk" |
| Declining extra work         | "I'd like to help, but I'm currently at capacity. Let me **flag** this with [manager] to **prioritize** accordingly" |
| Questioning a decision       | "Can we **revisit** this? I want to **ensure** we've considered [X] before we **commit**" |
| Saying no to a client        | "While we understand your request, there are some **constraints** we need to consider. Let's explore **alternatives** that can meet your needs" |
| Challenging data             | "These numbers don't quite **add up** — can we **look into** the methodology before we move forward?" |
| Resisting scope creep        | "This is a great idea, but it falls outside the current **scope**. Let's **table** this for the next phase" |

### Key Phrases for Boundaries

- "I want to **push back** on this — not to be difficult, but to **ensure** we're set up for success"
- "Let me **play devil's advocate** here for a moment"
- "I have some **reservations** about this approach — specifically around [X]"
- "I think we're **barking up the wrong tree** here"
- "We might be **biting off more than we can chew** with this"

## 4. Giving & Receiving Feedback

Use these in 1-on-1s, performance reviews, and code reviews.

### Giving Constructive Feedback

| Instead of... | Say... |
|--------------|--------|
| "This is wrong" | "I noticed an area where we could **refine** the approach" |
| "You need to do better" | "I'd like to see us **elevate** the quality in this area" |
| "Fix this" | "Could you **revisit** this section and **address** the [specific issue]?" |
| "You always miss deadlines" | "I've noticed the timeline has **slipped** a few times — let's **identify** what's causing the **bottleneck**" |
| "Good job" | "You **demonstrated** strong [skill] — particularly in how you [specific action]" |
| "This isn't good enough" | "This has a solid foundation, but I think we can **optimize** it further by [specific suggestion]" |

### Receiving Feedback

| Situation | What to Say |
|-----------|------------|
| Accepting criticism | "I appreciate you **flagging** that. I'll **address** it and **get back to you** with the updated version" |
| Asking for specifics | "Can you **elaborate** on what specifically you'd like me to **adjust**?" |
| Buying time to think | "That's a valid point. Let me **reflect** on it and come back with a plan" |

## 5. Negotiation & Persuasion

Use these when proposing ideas, asking for resources, or influencing decisions.

### Persuasion Phrases

| What You Want | How to Frame It |
|--------------|----------------|
| Propose a new idea | "I'd like to **put forward** a proposal that could **streamline** our process and **reduce** turnaround by [X]%" |
| Ask for budget | "To **deliver** optimal results, I'd **recommend** we **allocate** additional budget to [area]" |
| Pitch a change | "Based on the data, I believe we should **pivot** our approach — here's why it would **move the needle**" |
| Get buy-in | "This is a **win-win** — it **addresses** [stakeholder concern] while **accelerating** our [goal]" |
| Counter an objection | "I understand the concern. Let me **walk you through** how we **mitigate** that risk" |
| Ask for a raise | "Based on my contributions — specifically [X, Y, Z] — I'd like to **discuss** adjusting my **compensation** to reflect the value I **bring to the table**" |

### Power Idioms for Influence

- "Let's **get the ball rolling** on this"
- "This could be a **game changer** for our team"
- "We need to **raise the bar** on [X]"
- "I think we should **take the bull by the horns** here"
- "Let's **cut to the chase** — here's what I'm proposing"
- "We need to **move the needle** on this metric"

## 6. Small Talk & Connection

Use these at networking events, team socials, coffee chats, and the start/end of meetings.

### Conversation Starters (Beyond "How are you?")

| Context              | What to Say |
|----------------------|------------|
| Starting a meeting    | "Before we dive in — how's everyone's week going?" |
| New team member       | "Welcome aboard! What were you working on before joining us?" |
| Conference/event      | "What brought you to this event? Are you finding it valuable?" |
| After a presentation  | "I really liked your point about [X] — have you seen that work in practice?" |
| Coffee chat           | "What's keeping you busy these days — any interesting projects?" |
| Reconnecting          | "It's great to **reconnect** — what have you been up to since we last spoke?" |

### Transitions & Exits

- "It was great **catching up** — let's **touch base** again soon"
- "I don't want to **take up** too much of your time, but this was really insightful"
- "Let's **keep in touch** — I'd love to **follow up** on what we discussed"
- "I'll **drop you a line** next week to continue this conversation"

### Connection Power Words

| Word               | Use When... |
|--------------------|------------|
| **Resonate**       | Showing agreement ("That really **resonates** with me") |
| **Insightful**     | Complimenting ideas ("That's a really **insightful** perspective") |
| **Collaboratively** | Signaling teamwork ("I'd love to work on this **collaboratively**") |
| **Rapport**        | Building trust ("Building **rapport** with clients is key") |

## 10. Polishing the Thought Process

Use these to sound more collaborative and professional when you are processing information in real-time.

| Instead of... | Say... | Why It's Stronger |
|--------------|--------|-------------------|
| "I am thinking" / "Thinking..." | "Let me **think out loud** for a moment..." | Signals collaborative brainstorm mode |
| "Let me think" | "Let's **deliberate** on that for a second..." | Sounds more analytical and formal |
| "I'm still thinking" | "I'm still **processing** that information – give me a moment to **assess**" | Professional way to buy time |
| "I don't know" | "I don't have that detail on hand, let me **verify** and **get back to you**" | Shows accountability and commitment to accuracy |
| "I'm confused" | "Could you **elaborate** on that? I want to **ensure** I've **captured** the context" | Professional way to ask for clarification |
| "I'm lost" | "Let's **recalibrate** – I've lost the thread on the [specific point]" | High-status way to admit confusion |
| "Wait a minute" | "Let's **pause** here to **align** on the core objective" | Redirects the group strategically |
| "I think we can do it" | "I **envision** a path where we can **implement** this by..." | Shows vision and leadership |
| "I'll try" | "I will **attempt** to **expedite** this, though I want to **mitigate** any risks" | Sets realistic expectations with power words |
| "I agree" | "I **concur** with that assessment" / "That **resonates** with me" | Sophisticated agreement |

## 12. High-Touch Etiquette & Politeness

Small tweaks that make you sound more polished, respectful, and less abrupt.

| Instead of...       | Say... | Why It's Stronger |
|---------------------|--------|-------------------|
| "I disagree"        | "I represent a **different perspective** on this..." / "I have some **concerns** regarding..." | Disagrees without being confrontational |
| "I forgot"          | "That **slipped my mind** momentarily; thank you for the reminder." / "I must have **overlooked** that detail." | Takes ownership without sounding incompetent |
| "Change this"       | "Could you please **modify** this section?" / "Let's **refine** this part to align with [goal]." | Turns a command into a request |
| "I'm leaving"       | "I'm **heading out** for the day." / "I need to **drop off** now." | Softer, friendlier exit |
| "What?"             | "Could you **clarify** that point?" / "I missed that last part, could you **repeat**?" | Maintains professional demeanor |
| "It's hard"         | "This presents a **unique challenge**, but we can navigate it by..." | Reframes difficulty as a challenge |
| "I'm busy"          | "I'm currently **at capacity** with [Project X]." | Professional boundary setting |
