# Part 2 — Corporate Idioms & Expressions Guide

## Project Management & Execution

### Move the needle

**Meaning:** This means to make significant progress or achieve a meaningful result

**Situation:** You're reviewing sales figures and want to discuss strategies for improvement. You might say, "Our sales numbers haven't budged much this quarter. We need some innovative ideas to really move the needle.

---

### Proactive

**Meaning:** This describes someone who takes initiative and anticipates problems or needs before they arise

**Situation:** You're praising an employee for their excellent work. You can say, "I appreciate how proactive you were in identifying that potential client issue and resolving it before it became a problem”

---

### Low-hanging fruit

**Meaning:** This refers to easy tasks or quick wins that can be accomplished with minimal effort

**Situation:** You're delegating tasks to your team and want to assign some easier ones first. You can say, "Let's start by tackling the low-hanging fruit, like scheduling the conference call and gathering basic client information."

---

### Actionable items

**Meaning:** These are specific tasks or steps that need to be completed after a meeting or discussion

**Situation:** You're wrapping up a meeting about a product launch. You can say, "To summarize, our actionable items are finalizing the marketing materials and scheduling a training session for the sales team."

---

### Get the ball rolling

**Meaning:** This means to initiate or start something

**Situation:** You're in a meeting and everyone agrees a new marketing campaign is needed. You can say, "Great, so let's get the ball rolling by brainstorming some initial ideas."

---

### Drill down

**Meaning:** This means to investigate something in more detail

**Situation:** You're reviewing a complex financial report and need to understand a specific line item. You can ask your colleague, "Can you drill down a bit more on the marketing expenses for this quarter?"

---

### Benchmark

**Meaning:** This is a standard or reference point against which something can be measured or compared

**Situation:** You're evaluating the performance of your marketing campaign. You can say, "We'll use industry benchmarks to measure the effectiveness of our campaign and identify areas for improvement."

---

### Best practices

**Meaning:** These are the recognized and recommended ways of completing a task or achieving a goal within a particular industry.

**Situation:** You're training a new team member on customer service protocols. You can explain, "When interacting with clients, it's important to follow industry best practices to ensure a positive customer experience."

---

### Future-proof

**Meaning:** This describes something designed to remain relevant and effective in the anticipated future

**Situation:** You're discussing the company's IT infrastructure. You can say, "Investing in cloud based solutions will future-proof our systems and ensure they can adapt to evolving technological needs."

---

### Deep dive

**Meaning:** This signifies a thorough and detailed exploration of a particular topic or issue

**Situation:** You're preparing for a presentation on a new competitor. You can tell your team, "Let's do a deep dive into their marketing strategy and identify their strengths and weaknesses."

---

### Actionable insights

**Meaning:** This refers to discoveries or information that can be directly applied to improve a situation or make better decisions

**Situation:** You're reviewing customer feedback data. You can say, "We need to analyze this data to extract actionable insights that can help us enhance the customer experience."

---

### Sweat the details

**Meaning:** This means to pay close attention to minor details and ensure everything is done thoroughly

**Situation:** As you near a product launch, you can remind your team, "It's important to sweat the details now to avoid any glitches on launch day."

---

### Wrangle (something in)

**Meaning:** This means to bring something under control or get it completed successfully, often through focused effort

**Situation:** As project manager, you might say, "We need to wrangle in all the outstanding tasks before the deadline."

---

### Break down

**Meaning:** This means to divide a large task into smaller, more manageable steps.

**Situation:** You can say in a meeting, "Let's break down this project into milestones to track our progress."

---

### Push through

**Meaning:** This means to overcome any obstacles

**Situation:** Your team is facing a tight deadline for a project. You can say, "We have a lot to accomplish, but let's push through and get it done on time."

---

### In the pipeline

**Meaning:** Refers to projects or tasks that are currently being worked on or planned for the future

**Situation:** When discussing upcoming initiatives, you might say, "We have several exciting projects in the pipeline that will help us expand our market reach."

---

### Carry out

**Meaning:** This means to execute a plan

**Situation:** You're presenting a project proposal. You can say, "Once approved, we will carry out the project in three phases, with clear timelines and deliverables for each stage."

---

### Follow through

**Meaning:** This means to complete a task

**Situation:** You've been assigned a task in a meeting. You can demonstrate your commitment by saying, "I'll definitely follow through on this and provide an update by the end of the week."

---

### Hit the ground running

**Meaning:** This means to start working quickly and efficiently

**Situation:** If you've been assigned a task and want to show your commitment to getting started quickly, you can let your manager know by saying: "I received your email about the report. I've reviewed the details and I'm ready to hit the ground running on it."

---

### Streamlining

**Meaning:** Improving efficiency by simplifying processes or eliminating unnecessary steps

**Situation:** Your team is struggling with project management due to heavy workflow. You can say, "Let's streamline our project management process by adopting a centralized communication platform to enhance collaboration and reduce unnecessary delays.”

---

### Cut back

**Meaning:** To reduce the amount of something, such as expenses or resources

**Situation:** Your company is facing budget constraints. You can say, "In order to meet our financial goals, we'll need to cut back on non essential expenses and prioritize spending."

---

### Phase in

**Meaning:** To introduce or implement something gradually over a period of time

**Situation:** Your company is rolling out a new software system. You can say, "Instead of implementing the software all at once, let's phase it in department by department to minimize disruptions and ensure a smooth transition."

---

### Cut corners

**Meaning:** To take shortcuts or reduce costs by sacrificing quality or standard

**Situation:** Your team is under pressure to meet project deadlines. You can say, "We can't afford to cut corners on quality, even if it means working extra hours to deliver a top-notch product."

---

### Get down to brass tacks

**Meaning:** To focus on the essential or important details of a discussion or decision

**Situation:** Your team is discussing project budgets. You can say, "Let's get down to brass tacks and review the numbers to ensure we're staying within budget constraints."

---

### Nail down (something)

**Meaning:** To finalize details, secure an agreement, or lock something in place

**Situation:** You can use this after negotiations with a client. "We were finally able to nail down the contract terms with the client."

---

### Dead end

**Meaning:** A path or road that has no exit and leads nowhere

**Situation:** Discussing budget constraints with another department, you might say, "I understand your concerns; we're all in the same boat when it comes to tight budgets."

---

### Leave no stone unturned

**Meaning:** It means to do everything possible in your search for something or to solve a problem

**Situation:** Critical project files have gone missing from the shared drive. You're tasked with finding them. You could assure your manager, "We'll leave no stone unturned in our search for the missing files. We'll check all backups, user downloads, and contact IT for assistance."

---

### Come up with something

**Meaning:** It describes a short, intense brainstorming session where everyone throws out ideas rapidly

**Situation:** Your company is facing a decline in sales. Management is looking for innovative solutions. You could suggest a brainstorming session where everyone is encouraged to "think outside the box" and come up with unconventional marketing strategies.

---

### Strike while iron is hot
