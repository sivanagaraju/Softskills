Decision making is the process of making choices by identifying a decision, gathering information, and assessing alternative resolutions. While Critical Thinking analyzes the "what" and "why," Decision Making focuses on the "how" and "when" to take action.

## üìÅ Sub-Folder Overview

### [01_Foundations_of_Decision_Making](./01_Foundations_of_Decision_Making)

**Objective:** Understanding the core process of choice.

- Identifying the decision to be made.
- Setting criteria for success (What does a "good" decision look like?).
- Distinguishing between reversible and irreversible decisions (Type 1 vs. Type 2 decisions).

### [02_Decision_Models_and_Frameworks](./02_Decision_Models_and_Frameworks)

**Objective:** Using structured tools to reach a conclusion.

- **Weighted Scoring Models:** Comparing options against multiple criteria.
- **Decision Trees:** Mapping out outcomes and probabilities.
- **WRAP Framework (Chip & Dan Heath):** Widen your options, Reality-test your assumptions, Attain distance before deciding, Prepare to be wrong.

### [03_Quantitative_vs_Qualitative_Analysis](./03_Quantitative_vs_Qualitative_Analysis)

**Objective:** Balancing data with intuition and values.

- **Cost-Benefit Analysis:** Measuring the financial and non-financial impact.
- **Pros and Cons Lists:** When they work and why they often fail.
- **Intuitive Decision Making:** When to trust your "gut" (Expert intuition vs. emotional reaction).

### [04_Group_Decision_Making_and_Consensus](./04_Group_Decision_Making_and_Consensus)

**Objective:** Managing decisions involving multiple stakeholders.

- **The RACI Matrix:** Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed.
- **Avoiding Groupthink:** Strategies for encouraging dissent and diversity.
- **Consensus-building vs. Dictatorial decision making:** Pros and cons of each style.

### [05_High_Ambiguity_and_Architectural_Risk](./05_High_Ambiguity_and_Architectural_Risk)

**Objective:** (Principal Level) Making multi-year technical bets.

- **Risk Assessment:** Impact vs. Probability in complex systems.
- **Navigating Ambiguity:** Moving forward when there is no "correct" answer.
- **The "Pre-Mortem" Technique:** Imagining system failure to identify current architectural risks.

### [06_Psychology_of_Choice_and_Decision_Fatigue](./06_Psychology_of_Choice_and_Decision_Fatigue)

**Objective:** Understanding the human limits of choosing.

- **Decision Fatigue:** Why making too many small choices ruins big ones.
- **Analysis Paralysis:** How to overcome the fear of making the "wrong" choice.
- **Heuristics in Decision Making:** Mental shortcuts we use to speed up the process.

### [07_Post_Decision_Evaluation_and_Learning](./07_Post_Decision_Evaluation_and_Learning)

**Objective:** Improving the process by looking at results.

- **Decision Logs:** Keeping a record of why choices were made.
- **Outcome vs. Process:** Why a good decision can have a bad outcome (and vice versa).
- **After-Action Reviews (AAR):** Learning from successes and failures.
