### Internal Family Systems (IFS) â€” Richard Schwartz

**What it is:** IFS is a therapeutic model that treats the psyche as a system of "parts" â€” each with its own perspective, function, and emotional age. Trigger reactions are understood as the activation of a specific "part" â€” often a protective part trying to prevent a wounded "exile" part from being exposed.

**Why it directly matters for trigger work:**
When your shame trigger fires and you snap at a colleague â€” from an IFS perspective, that reactive snap is a "manager" or "firefighter" part doing its job of protecting the "exile" (the young you who learned shame was dangerous). The reaction is not pathological. It is a protective system working as designed.

**The IFS insight that changes how you view your triggers:**
Every triggered reaction is a *protective behaviour* from a part of you that is trying to keep a wound safe. Working with the reaction from this lens â€” with curiosity rather than judgment â€” accelerates the awareness work.

**How to access:** Read *No Bad Parts* by Richard Schwartz (most accessible entry point). For deep work, an IFS-trained therapist is significantly more effective than solo application.

### Polyvagal Theory â€” Stephen Porges

**What it is:** Porges' framework identifies three levels of the autonomic nervous system response to threat, mediated by the vagus nerve:

| Level | State | Experience |
|---|---|---|
| **Ventral Vagal** (safe) | Social engagement | Connected, calm, open, curious |
| **Sympathetic** (threat) | Fight or Flight | Anxious, defensive, reactive |
| **Dorsal Vagal** (overwhelm) | Shutdown / Freeze | Collapsed, numb, disconnected, dissociated |

**Why it matters for trigger work:**
Understanding *which level* you drop to in different trigger situations gives you more specific, targeted regulation strategies:

- Fight/Flight responses â†’ Sympathetic state â†’ vagal breathing, cold water, slow movement
- Freeze/Shutdown responses â†’ Dorsal Vagal state â†’ gentle movement, temperature change, social engagement (safe person, gentle voice)

**How to access:** *The Polyvagal Theory in Therapy* by Deb Dana (more accessible than Porges' academic writing). This is the framework used in the S.T.O.P. breathing practice â€” specifically the extended exhale for vagal activation.
