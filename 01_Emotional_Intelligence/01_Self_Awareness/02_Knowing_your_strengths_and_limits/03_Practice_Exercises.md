## Exercise 01 ‚Äî üîç The Strength Identification Deep Dive

### Goal

Move beyond surface-level "I'm good at presentations" to identify your true signature strengths ‚Äî activities where performance, energy, and authenticity all converge.

### Why This Exercise Exists

Most strengths lists are really **competency lists** ‚Äî things you can do well. This exercise filters for **true strengths**: activities that also energise you and feel authentically like you. The distinction changes development planning entirely.

### Instructions (45 minutes)

**Step 1 ‚Äî The Activity Recall (15 min)**
Think back across your professional history (last 3‚Äì5 years). List 8‚Äì10 moments or activities where you felt:

- Fully absorbed in the work
- Noticeably more energised during or after
- "This is exactly what I should be doing right now"
- Like the "best version of yourself"

Write them as brief descriptors: "Debugging the complex API issue," "Leading the workshop with the client team," "Writing the strategy memo," "Mentoring the junior hire."

**Step 2 ‚Äî The Pattern Extraction (15 min)**
For each activity, identify: *what was I actually doing at the core of this?*

| Activity | Core Action | VIA Strength Category (guess) |
|---|---|---|
| Debugging the complex API issue | Analysing a puzzle, finding a root cause | Curiosity, Love of Learning |
| Leading the workshop | Facilitating group thinking, holding space | Leadership, Social Intelligence |
| Writing the strategy memo | Making complex ideas clear and structured | Creativity, Judgment |
| Mentoring the junior hire | Helping someone see what they can become | Kindness, Perspective |

**Step 3 ‚Äî The Three-Filter Test (15 min)**
For your top 5 patterns, apply the three-criteria test:

| Strength Candidate | Performance (High/Medium/Low) | Energy (Energising/Neutral/Draining) | Authenticity (Real you/Performed) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | | ‚úÖ True Strength / ‚ö†Ô∏è Learned Competency / üî∂ Developing |
| | | | | |

**Step 4 ‚Äî The VIA Verification**
Take the VIA Survey ([viacharacter.org](https://www.viacharacter.org)) if you haven't yet. Compare your top 5 VIA results to your patterns from Step 2. Note where they align and where they diverge.

### üåü What a Strong Response Looks Like

> *"My three-filter test revealed: I thought 'presenting to executives' was a strength. Energy filter caught it ‚Äî I'm drained after every senior presentation, even when I do well. Verdict: Learned Competency. I'm good at it. It's not a strength. What actually passed all three filters: facilitated problem-solving in small groups (high performance, reliably energising, feels most like me). That's a true strength ‚Äî and my current role gives me maybe 5% of my week doing it."*

## Exercise 02 ‚Äî ‚ö° Two-Week Energy Audit

### Goal

Build an empirical, observation-based picture of which tasks energise and which drain you ‚Äî replacing retrospective impression with systematic real-time data.

### Setup (Before Week 1 Starts)

Create a log in any format ‚Äî phone notes, Notion table, or paper journal divided into two columns: *Task* and *Energy After (E = Energised / N = Neutral / D = Drained / SD = Significantly Drained).*

Optional: add a 5-word "why" note (e.g., "SD ‚Äî repetitive, no thinking," "E ‚Äî solved something hard").

### During the Two Weeks (3√ó daily)

After every significant work block (not every 5 minutes ‚Äî after meaningful chunks of work), record the task and its energy impact. Takes approximately 15 seconds each time.

Set a phone reminder for 10 AM, 2 PM, and 5 PM if helpful.

### Week 2 Analysis (30 minutes)

**Part A ‚Äî Frequency count**

| Energy Level | Number of Work Blocks | % of Total | Most Common Task Types |
|---|---|---|---|
| Energised (E) | | | |
| Neutral (N) | | | |
| Drained (D) | | | |
| Significantly Drained (SD) | | | |

**Part B ‚Äî The Pattern Questions**

1. What do your TOP 5 most-energising tasks have in common? (Type of work? Who you work with? What skill is being used? Level of complexity? Autonomy?)

2. What do your TOP 5 most-draining tasks have in common? (Same questions.)

3. What percentage of your week is currently in the Drained or Significantly Drained category?
   - Under 15%: Healthy
   - 15‚Äì30%: Yellow zone ‚Äî build management strategies
   - Over 30%: High disengagement and burnout risk ‚Äî renegotiation needed

4. Is there a task in your Drained column that you are also doing frequently (multiple times per week)? This is your highest-priority delegation or renegotiation candidate.

### üåü What a Strong Response Looks Like

> *"Week 2 analysis: 34% of my week lands in Drained or Significantly Drained. The most draining items: status report writing (twice weekly, ~3h total), recurring vendor management calls (once a week, SD every time), and detailed spreadsheet reconciliation (ad hoc but frequent). All three are things I do adequately ‚Äî none are things that use my top strengths. I brought this data to my manager and proposed: shift status reports to a collaborative team doc (reducing my time from 3h to 45min), delegate vendor call note-taking to the coordinator. She agreed. Over the following month my energy balance shifted from 34% drained to 19%."*

## Exercise 03 ‚Äî üîç The Blind Spot Hunt (Informal 360¬∞)

### Goal

Access your Blind Spot Johari quadrant ‚Äî what others observe in you that you cannot see yourself ‚Äî through structured, direct requests for honest feedback.

### Why "Informal" ‚Äî And Why It's Still Rigorous

An organisational 360¬∞ tool takes weeks to design and deploy. This exercise achieves a similar level of insight through direct, well-framed conversations or written questions. The key to making it work: the quality of your questions and the explicit permission you give people to be honest.

### Instructions

**Step 1 ‚Äî Select Your 5 Respondents**
Choose people who:

- Work closely enough with you to have real observations
- Come from different contexts (not just your immediate team)
- Include at least one person who has given you feedback that surprised you in the past

**Step 2 ‚Äî Write or Ask These Exact Questions**

Option A ‚Äî In conversation (coffee or 1:1):
> *"I'm doing some deliberate self-development work and I'd genuinely value your input. I want to understand what you observe in how I work, from your perspective. There are no wrong answers and I genuinely want honesty. Can I ask you a few questions?"*
>
> 1. "What do you think I do exceptionally well ‚Äî that I might undervalue?"
> 2. "Is there one thing you'd genuinely like me to do differently in how we work together?"
> 3. "Is there a pattern you notice in how I show up ‚Äî something I may not be fully aware of?"

Option B ‚Äî Written (Google Form or email):
> Send the same three questions with this header: *"I'm taking my development seriously and I need honest input. Your name will not be seen by anyone else and your feedback will only be used for my personal development. Please tell me the truth."*

**Step 3 ‚Äî Record All Responses Without Editing**

Write down everything that was said ‚Äî including things that don't land well initially. Don't filter for what's comfortable.

**Step 4 ‚Äî Pattern Analysis**

| Theme | Mentioned by How Many People? | Their Specific Words | My Emotional Reaction | Is This in My Blind Spot? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | | |

**Step 5 ‚Äî The Emotional Reaction Signal**

Note your pattern of emotional reaction to each theme:

- **Defensive / "That's not fair":** Likely in the Blind Spot ‚Äî the defensiveness is a signal that this truth is landing somewhere real
- **"That surprises me ‚Äî I didn't realise":** Confirmed Blind Spot
- **"I knew that about myself":** This is Hidden (you know it, now it's in Open)
- **"This is familiar ‚Äî others have said this":** This is in the Open quadrant (probably already known)

### üåü What a Strong Response Looks Like

**Theme identified:** "Two people independently said I sometimes seem impatient in discussions when someone takes a long time to reach their point."

**Emotional reaction:** Mild defensiveness ‚Äî "I'm not impatient, I'm efficient."

**The signal:** The defensiveness itself confirms this is landing somewhere real. My experience of myself ("efficient") and their experience of me ("impatient") are divergent ‚Äî that's the Blind Spot.

**The practice elected:** For 30 days, in every meeting where I notice the urge to speed someone along ‚Äî I pause, breathe, and let them finish without interrupting. At the end of the month, I ask one of the feedback-givers: "Have you noticed any difference?"

## Exercise 04 ‚Äî üó∫Ô∏è The Limits Diagnostic

### Goal

Correctly type each of your current limits ‚Äî distinguishing skill gaps (develop), energy drains (manage), and values conflicts (examine) ‚Äî so you can apply the right response to each.

### Instructions (20 minutes per limit)

**Step 1 ‚Äî List Your Current 5 Most Significant Professional Limits**

Write them honestly. These are areas where you underperform, avoid, or feel stuck:

1. ___________________________
2. ___________________________
3. ___________________________
4. ___________________________
5. ___________________________

**Step 2 ‚Äî Type Each Limit Using the Diagnostic Questions**

For each limit, answer these three questions:

```
Q1: "If I had focused training, coaching, and practice for 6 months ‚Äî 
     could I develop genuine competency here?"
‚Üí YES = likely a Skill Gap
‚Üí "I already can do it adequately" = Move to Q2

Q2: "When I do this task and do it reasonably well ‚Äî 
     am I typically drained afterward? (Not just when it goes badly)"
‚Üí YES = likely Energy Drain
‚Üí NO ‚Üí Move to Q3

Q3: "Is there a sense of 'wrongness' or identity conflict when I do this ‚Äî 
     not discomfort but moral or identity resistance?"
‚Üí YES = likely Values Conflict
```

**Step 3 ‚Äî Build the Limit Response Plan**

| Limit | Type | Response Strategy | Specific Next Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| | Skill Gap | Deliberate practice plan | Identify specific learning goal + when |
| | Energy Drain | Delegation / renegotiation | Identify who or how + timeline |
| | Values Conflict | Naming + deeper conversation | Write the conflict precisely; discuss with manager |
| | | | |

### üåü What a Strong Response Looks Like

**Limit identified:** "Running stakeholder alignment meetings"

**Q1:** "Could I get better with training?" ‚Üí Yes, probably. But I already run them adequately.
**Q2:** "Am I drained after even good ones?" ‚Üí Yes, every time. Even when it goes well.
**Q3:** "Is there identity/moral resistance?" ‚Üí No. It just empties me.

**Type: Energy Drain**

**Response:** Batch all stakeholder alignment meetings to Tuesday afternoons only (energy is higher Tuesday; batching reduces the frequency of recovery cycles). Delegate pre-meeting agenda preparation to the project manager to reduce my prep burden. After 4 weeks: reassess whether the drain reduction is sufficient.

## Exercise 05 ‚Äî üî≤ The Johari Mapping Session

### Goal

Use all data collected from Exercises 01‚Äì04 to build a comprehensive, named Johari Window for your current professional context.

### Prerequisites

At minimum: complete Exercise 01 (strengths) and Exercise 03 (blind spot hunt). This exercise is the synthesis.

### Instructions (60‚Äì90 minutes)

**Step 1 ‚Äî Build the Four Quadrants**

Draw or create a 2√ó2 grid. Label the four quadrants. Populate each with *specific, named observations* ‚Äî not vague categories:

**OPEN (Known to me, confirmed by others):**

- My strength data from Exercise 01 that 360¬∞ respondents also mentioned
- Skills that are widely acknowledged by peers and that I also recognise
*(e.g., "Structured written communication; pattern identification in complex datasets; mentoring effectiveness")*

**BLIND SPOT (Surfaced by Exercise 03, new to me):**

- What others mentioned that surprised me
- Patterns I hadn't seen in myself
*(e.g., "Appearing impatient in meetings; tendency to resolve others' thinking for them rather than letting them arrive there; visible discomfort with emotional conversations")*

**HIDDEN (I know, but haven't shared or disclosed):**

- Vulnerabilities I don't show professionally
- Strengths I downplay or don't claim
- Fears or concerns I hold internally
*(e.g., "Uncertainty about my direction; latent interest in people development; anxiety about high-visibility public presentations")*

**UNKNOWN (Neither me nor others see clearly):**

- Responses to contexts I've never been in
- Potential in domains I haven't explored
- Deep patterns that therapy, coaching, or major life events might eventually surface

**Step 2 ‚Äî Prioritise Actions by Quadrant**

| Quadrant | What's There | My Single Most Important Action |
|---|---|---|
| Blind Spot | | Address the 1 pattern with highest professional impact |
| Hidden | | 1 selective disclosure that would build a key relationship |
| Unknown | | 1 new experience or challenge to explore potential |
