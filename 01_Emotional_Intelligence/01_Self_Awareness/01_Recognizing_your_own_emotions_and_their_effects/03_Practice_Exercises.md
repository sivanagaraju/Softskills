## ‚ö° Exercise 1 ‚Äî Emotion Word Swap (Daily, 1 min)

**Goal:** Build emotional vocabulary ‚Äî stop using vague words like "fine", "okay", "stressed".

**How to do it:**
> Every time you are about to say **"I'm fine"** or **"I'm stressed"** ‚Äî pause and replace it with a more precise word.

**Practice right now:**

Replace each vague word below with 3 more precise alternatives:

| Vague Word | Your Precise Alternatives |
|---|---|
| Fine | ___________ / ___________ / ___________ |
| Stressed | ___________ / ___________ / ___________ |
| Upset | ___________ / ___________ / ___________ |
| Good | ___________ / ___________ / ___________ |

*(Reference answers: Fine ‚Üí Content/Relieved/Neutral | Stressed ‚Üí Overwhelmed/Anxious/Pressured)*

## ‚ö° Exercise 2 ‚Äî Body Scan Check-In (3x Daily, 2 min)

**Goal:** Develop the habit of catching emotions in your body before they escalate.

**Reference ‚Äî Where Do You Feel It?**

![Emotional Body Map ‚Äî Scientific Sensation Chart](https://media.post.rvohealth.io/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2020/09/187598-Not-Sure-What-You%25E2%2580%2599re-Feeling_-Maybe-This-Body-Chart-Will-Help-1296x3223-Body-2-scaled.jpg)

**Set 3 phone alarms:** Morning (9am) | Afternoon (2pm) | Evening (8pm)

When the alarm goes off, answer these 3 questions:

```
1. PAUSE ‚Äî What am I physically feeling right now?
   ‚Üí Head? Shoulders? Chest? Stomach? Hands?
   
2. NAME IT ‚Äî What emotion matches this sensation?
   ‚Üí _______________________________________________
   
3. TRACE IT ‚Äî What event/thought triggered this?
   ‚Üí _______________________________________________
```

> ‚úÖ **Success marker:** After 2 weeks, you should start noticing emotions *without* needing the alarm.

## ‚ö° Exercise 5 ‚Äî The Mirror Exercise (Weekly, 5 min)

**Goal:** Recognize emotions through facial expressions ‚Äî your own and others'.

**How:**

1. Sit quietly in front of a mirror (or use your phone camera)
2. Think of a recent event that triggered an emotion
3. **Observe your face** ‚Äî what does the emotion look like on you?
4. Practice **consciously labeling** the expression: "This is what my frustration looks like"

> üí° **Why this works:** Most people have never observed their own emotional expressions. This builds the muscle of recognizing subtle cues ‚Äî both in yourself and later in others.
